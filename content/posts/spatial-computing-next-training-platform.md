Spatial computing moves training off the flat screen and into the body. Learners
stop watching a procedure and start performing it, with their hands, in a space
that responds the way the real one does.

## Why now

Headsets crossed the price and comfort thresholds that kept VR out of day-to-day
operations. The hardware finally disappears far enough that the *task* is what you
remember, not the device on your face.

## What changes

- **Retention** climbs when practice is embodied rather than observed.
- **Risk** drops to zero — a trainee can fail an emergency-response drill safely.
- **Measurement** comes built in: every interaction is a data point you can defend.

That combination is why we treat spatial computing as a training platform, not a
demo.
