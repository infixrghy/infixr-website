The point of a simulation is to let people fail where failure is free. A good
safe-to-fail design makes mistakes obvious, recoverable, and instructive.

## Three rules we follow

- **Make the failure visible.** If a wrong action has no consequence, it teaches
  nothing.
- **Make recovery possible.** Dead-ends frustrate; a path back keeps learners in
  the loop.
- **Make the lesson land at the moment of failure**, not in a debrief an hour later.

Failure is the feature. Design for it deliberately.
